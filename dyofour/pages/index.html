Must-Reads!

  • Invisible Life of Addie LaRue
    The setting of the book is in France, the year of 1714. The main character Addie LaRue hated her life in her small village. She makes a deal after dark. This deal leads Addie to live an exciting life. Addie is a memorable woman that can never be remembered. Around her 300th birthday, Addie meets someone who changes her life, simply by being able to remember her name.
  • A Good Girl's Guide to Murder
    Andie Bell was murder by Sal Singh, everyone knew it was him. Pippa Fitz-Amobi decides to crack open the case on her lingering doubt. What started out as a harmless topic for her final year project turned into a case that had more secrets than noted. She discovers that the real killer is still free, what will this killer do to keep Pippa quiet?
  • A Tree Grows In Brooklyn
    A Tree Grows in Brooklyn is about a young girl growing up at the turn of the century. Francis (Francie) Nolan did not grow up fortunate, she was faced with many challenges many little girls shouldn't have to deal with. That said, this never brought down the spirits of the Nolan family, they had little but a surplus of love and trust in each other.
  • American Serial Killers
    Peter Vronsky has established himself as the foremost expert on the history of serial killers. In the years 1950-2000 there was an epidemic of serial killers in America. You dive into cases like Ted Bundy and the Golden State Killer. The book also includes perennial favorites like Ed Kemper and Jeffery Dahmer, along with lesser-known cases like Melvin Rees and Harvey Glatman.
  • Mindhunter 
    Special Agent John Douglas is a model for law enforcement. John Douglas shaped new ideas of behavioral science and criminal profiling. This book is about his experience in his 25 years of service. John Douglas would interview and research serial killers and assassins, a few notorious names you may recognize include: Charles Manson, Richard Speck, John Wayne Gacy, and James Earl Ray. He wanted to reach into their minds and understand what motivated them to do what they did. Joh Douglas also hunted the sadistic criminals: The Trailside Killer in San Francisco, and the Atlanta Child murder.
  • 1st to Die
    A group of women with all different professions come together to solve crime. One homicide inspector, one medical examiner, one assistant D.A., and one crime journalist of the San Francisco Chronicle. These four women form the Women’s Murder Club. In a 22 book series these women kick butt solving crime.
  • The Ex Hex
    Vivienne Jones had her heart broken by her boyfriend… nine years ago. To this day she still suffers from heartache. Vivienne Jones has a secret, she is a witch. Though she doesn’t use her magic she mixes magic and alcohol, innocently curses that awful but dreamy Rhys Penhallow. One day, he unexpectedly showed up in their town Graves Glen, Georgia. During the town’s annual fall festival her little joke curse might have done more damage than expected. Vivienne and Rhys, the unlikely pair, have to work together and fix some drunken mistakes.
  • The Wistera Society of Lady Scoundrels
    Cecilia Bassingwaite is the ideal Victorian lady. She is nothing short of beautiful, she’s also a thief. The Wisteria Society is a society of lady pirates flying in their houses around England. Some activities would include: drinking tea, blackmailing friends, and acquiring treasure by all means. One day Cecilia answered the knock at her overbearing aunt’s door that she resides with and met her fate. On the other side of the door was her assassin Ned Lightbourne. He was hired by Captain Morvath, a pirate who desires to rid England of all its presumptuous women. Unfortunately, for Captain Morvath, he underestimated this society of women, especially Cecilia. Fortunately, for Ned Lightbourne, he underestimated this society of women, especially the lovely Cecilia.
